3 March 2026 | Prepared by Foxhue Studio

1

Executive Summary

London Laser Clinic (londonlaser.co.uk) operates across 7 London locations with a strong Google reputation (4.8 stars, 478+ reviews). However, the website has significant technical and SEO shortfalls that are handing organic traffic to competitors. The site runs on HTTP without SSL, scores 8/100 on Google PageSpeed, has no blog or content strategy, no structured data, and no social media links. These are issues that competitors have already addressed.

Key Findings

  • Critical performance gap: PageSpeed score of 8/100 (mobile) puts the site at severe risk of ranking penalties. Competitors like Pulse Light Clinic and SK:N score 40-70+.
  • No SSL certificate: The site runs on HTTP. Google has flagged non-HTTPS sites in search results since 2018, and Chrome shows "Not Secure" warnings to visitors.
  • Zero content strategy: No blog, no educational content, no service-area pages. Competitors are capturing high-intent search traffic with laser hair removal guides, treatment comparisons, and location-specific landing pages.
  • Missing local SEO signals: No schema markup, no location-specific pages for the 7 branches, and no social media presence linked from the website — despite having a YouTube channel with 43 videos.
  • Strong foundation to build on: 478 Google reviews at 4.8 stars, 7 physical locations, and an existing YouTube channel (771 subscribers) provide a solid base for growth.

Top 3 Priority Actions

Priority 1

Install SSL & Fix Site Speed

Migrate to HTTPS and optimise page load performance. These are table-stakes for Google ranking.

Priority 2

Add Location Landing Pages

Create dedicated pages for each of the 7 locations with local schema markup to capture "near me" searches.

Priority 3

Launch a Content Strategy

Start publishing educational blog content targeting high-volume keywords like "laser hair removal London" and treatment FAQs.

Technical Recommendations

Install SSL Certificate (HTTPS)

The site currently runs on HTTP. An SSL certificate is essential for Google ranking, user trust, and Chrome's "Secure" indicator. Most hosting providers offer free SSL via Let's Encrypt.

High impact — Quick win

Improve Page Load Speed

PageSpeed score of 8/100 is critically low. Key actions: compress and lazy-load images, minify CSS/JS, enable browser caching, consider a CDN, and upgrade hosting if shared.

High impact — 2-4 weeks

Add Schema Markup

Implement LocalBusiness, MedicalBusiness, and Service schema (JSON-LD) on every page. This helps Google display rich results including ratings, opening hours, and service areas.

High impact — Quick win

Mobile Performance Optimisation

Google uses mobile-first indexing. Ensure the site renders quickly on mobile devices: reduce DOM size, optimise fonts, eliminate render-blocking resources, and use responsive images.

High impact — 2-4 weeks

Integrate Booking on Website

The current Phorest booking requires users to leave the site. Embed the booking widget directly on service and location pages to reduce friction and improve conversion rates.

Medium impact — 1-2 weeks

Add Social Media Links

The clinic has a YouTube channel with 43 videos and 771 subscribers, but no social links appear on the website. Add social icons to the header/footer on every page.

Medium impact — Quick win

Fix Meta Tags & Title Tags

Ensure every page has unique, keyword-rich title tags and meta descriptions. Each location and service page should target specific search terms.

High impact — 1 week

Implement XML Sitemap

Create and submit an XML sitemap to Google Search Console. This ensures all pages — especially new location and blog pages — are discovered and indexed promptly.

Medium impact — Quick win

Content Strategy

Competitors like Premier Laser and SK:N publish 2-4 blog posts per month. Starting with 2 posts per month would establish momentum. Each post should target a specific keyword cluster.

"Laser Hair Removal: Your Complete Guide"

Pillar content covering how it works, what to expect, pain levels, number of sessions, skin types, and aftercare. Link from service pages.

Pillar content — 2,000+ words

Address the common concern about suitability for darker skin. Low competition keyword with high conversion intent from London's diverse population.

Niche authority — 1,200+ words

"How Much Does Laser Hair Removal Cost in London?"

Pricing transparency content. Google loves pricing pages, and searchers with price intent are close to booking. Include comparison with waxing/shaving long-term costs.

Commercial intent — 1,000+ words

"Soprano ICE vs IPL: Which is Better?"

Technology comparison post. Positions London Laser as an authority on treatment technology. Targets people researching options before booking.

Educational — 1,000+ words

Local SEO Checklist

With 7 locations across London, local SEO represents the biggest growth opportunity. Each action below should be completed for every location.

Google Business Profile

  • Verify all 7 Google Business Profiles are claimed and active
  • Add complete business information: hours, services, attributes, and description
  • Upload professional photos for each location (exterior, interior, staff, treatments)
  • Enable Google Messages and Q&A on each profile
  • Respond to all Google reviews (positive and negative) within 24-48 hours
  • Post Google Business updates weekly (offers, tips, before/after photos)

Location Pages

  • Create a dedicated landing page for each location (e.g. /locations/kensington)
  • Include embedded Google Map, address, phone, and opening hours on each
  • Add unique content per location (directions, nearby landmarks, parking info)
  • Implement LocalBusiness schema markup on each location page
  • Include location-specific testimonials where possible

Directory Listings

  • Ensure consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all directories
  • Claim listings on: Yell, Thomson Local, Yelp, Trustpilot, Treatwell
  • Add to industry-specific directories: Glowday, WhatClinic, RealSelf
  • Set up Apple Maps listings for all locations
  • Monitor and correct any inconsistent information across directories

Review Generation

  • Create a post-treatment review request process (email or SMS)
  • Add QR codes at reception linking to Google review page
  • Showcase reviews on the website (with schema markup for aggregate rating)
  • Consider Trustpilot or Reviews.io for verified review collection
